Anemia is a condition in which the body does not have enough healthy red blood cells.
Red blood cells carry oxygen to the body's tissues. Common symptoms of anemia include
fatigue, weakness, pale skin, shortness of breath, dizziness, and cold hands and feet.

Iron-deficiency anemia is the most common type, caused by a lack of iron in the body.
It can result from blood loss, poor diet, or the body's inability to absorb iron.
Treatment often includes iron supplements and eating iron-rich foods such as red meat,
beans, and leafy green vegetables.

Vitamin deficiency anemia can occur when the body lacks vitamin B12 or folate.
Severe or untreated anemia can lead to complications including heart problems and
extreme fatigue. Anyone with persistent symptoms should consult a doctor.
